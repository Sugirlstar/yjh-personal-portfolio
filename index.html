About me

I'm a 2nd-year PhD student in EAPS, Purdue University. I work with Dr. Lei Wang in the Weather and Climate Dynamics Laboratory. My research focus on extreme weather events and their underlying dynamical mechanisms.

What I'm Studying

  • Mid-latitude Dynamics

    Atmospheric blockings are large-scale, long-lasting wave anomalies that can cause various hazardous impacts. However, the dominant mechanisms driving their formation are not well understood.

  • Land-Atmosphere Interaction

    Land surface variables can strongly interact with atmosphere, potentially intensifying extreme weather events such as heatwaves and droughts. But to what extent do these interactions amplify such extremes? When compared to large-scale atmospheric processes, which factor plays the dominant role?

  • XAI

    Explainable AI (XAI) techniques are commonly applied in image processing. Since weather patterns share similar spatial features with images, XAI can also be leveraged to identify and predict extreme weather events while providing insights into their underlying mechanisms.
